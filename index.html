Performance

Notes: Carousels are the most prevalent common design element on the web. They're everywhere and they have a cool factor. I joke that they exist only to please design committees, but they really do seem to serve an important purpose - in an eye-catching way, promote content. A good carousel carries with it a lot of connotations about forward-thinking the library is, how modern, how digital, how not an old card catalog. On the 20 Great Public Library Websites, all of them? have carousels. For all the pizzazz and utility it adds there are costs. Consider the most obvious: a slideshow is a bunch of images, performance. Users expect .... Additionally, this expectation of speed has a one-for-one relationship with the user's efficiency at browsing. This efficiency is built on text and clutter and ad blindness. Navigating through the BS. Click rate for carousels is ridiculously low. People know this, so the idea is that they auto-scroll so that the third or fourth slide might slip into view. This in turn has a tremendous drawback on usbaility.
